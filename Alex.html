What I did

As part of my University modules, I developed numerous remotely controlled robot cars during my time in NUS. These cars are controlled either by apps on the phone, on the computer or sometimes are fully autonomous. I have worked with numerous microcontrollers and their relevant software.

Some of the functionality I implemented on the microcontroller software include obstacle avoidance, Bluetooth/wifi control and multithreading. For more specific features, I can dive deep and code in bare-metal for watchdog Timers, GPIO, etc.

For the hardware side, I've implemented LiDAR scanners, IR sensors and microphones. Overall, I would say I have become quite comfortable debugging circuitry and the code required to utilize them.
